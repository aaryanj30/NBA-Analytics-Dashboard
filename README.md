# NBA-Analytics-Dashboard
This project presents a comprehensive NBA analytics dashboard built using Power BI and DAX queries, designed by leveraging multi-season NBA data (2004–latest), and the dashboard transforms raw game and player statistics into actionable insights through interactive visualisations and storytelling.

## What this project explores
This dashboard is built to dig into a few key aspects of the NBA:
  -How scoring has changed over the years
  -The balance between player efficiency and scoring volume
  -How consistent (or inconsistent) teams are across seasons
  -How competitive the two conferences really are

## Why this matters:
  -Basketball today is heavily driven by data. Teams use analytics for strategy, scouts use it for player evaluation, and fans rely on it to understand the game better. 
  -This dashboard simplifies a complex dataset and makes it easy to explore, helping turn raw numbers into meaningful insights.

## Objectives
  -Understand how scoring trends have evolved over different seasons
  -Evaluate how players perform — not just how much they score, but how efficiently
  -Identify which teams are consistent and which are unpredictable
  -Compare how teams perform at home vs away
  -Analyze the competition between the Eastern and Western conferences
  -Build an interactive dashboard that lets users explore all of this on their own

## Key Features of the Dashboard
  🎛️ Easy-to-use filters for season, team, and player
  📈 Clear trend visuals to track performance over time
  🧮 KPI cards showing important metrics like points, win %, and efficiency
  🔍 Drill-down views — from league level to team level to player level
  ⚡ Fully interactive visuals that respond to user input
  🎨 Clean and intuitive design focused on storytelling

## Key Insights & Analysis
1. Scoring Trends: Scoring has steadily increased over the years, especially after 2015 — likely due to faster gameplay and more offensive strategies.
2. Team Dominance: A few teams consistently perform well across seasons, showing long-term strength in strategy and squad building.
3. Efficiency vs Volume: Scoring more doesn’t always mean better performance — the best players are those who balance high scoring with high efficiency.
4. Home Advantage: Teams tend to perform noticeably better at home, with around a 5–10% higher win rate.
5. Conference Comparison: The Western Conference appears more competitive overall, while the Eastern Conference has phases of dominance by a few top teams.
6. Consistency vs Volatility: Some teams maintain steady performance year after year, while others fluctuate a lot — reflecting instability in performance or strategy.

## Questions This Dashboard Answers
  -Which teams have been consistently strong over the years?
  -How has NBA scoring changed over time?
  -Who are the most efficient players vs just high scorers?
  -Does playing at home really make a difference?
  -Which conference is more competitive — East or West?
  -Which teams are stable performers and which are unpredictable?
  -How closely do rankings reflect actual performance?
  -How much do individual players impact team success?

## Tech Stack
  📊 Power BI for building the dashboard
  🧮 DAX for calculations and KPIs
  🧹 Power Query for cleaning and transforming data
  📂 CSV files used for structured data storage
  📐 Data modeling using relationships across multiple tables

📂 Dataset Overview
games.csv → Match-level data (scores, outcomes, teams), Player-level stats (points, assists, rebounds, etc.)
players.csv → Player information
teams.csv → Team details
ranking.csv → Season-wise rankings and standings
